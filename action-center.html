Letter Templates: Contact Power in 5 Minutes

We've made advocacy effortless with ready-to-send letters for Arizona officials. Our Independent Prison Oversight template demands full funding for the new Ombudsman's Office. The Drug Policy template calls for expanding Good Samaritan laws to protect 911 callers during overdoses. The Probation Reform template urges legislators to cap probation terms. Each template is customizable; just add your story. We've seen 300 letters generate legislative meetings; 1,000 letters move bills to committee.

For media advocacy, use our Op-Ed template to pitch local papers. We've had opinion pieces published using this framework, focusing on personal narrative + data + clear ask. The Core Civics & Advocacy Training team reviews drafts if you email them through our contact form.

All templates are available in English and Spanish, with plain-language instructions. Print versions are distributed weekly at community centers in Maryvale, Guadalupe, and South Tucson.

Volunteer Roles: Join Our Teams

We need volunteer prison oversight monitors to accompany our staff on facility inspections. This role requires a 6-month commitment, background clearance, and completion of our 16-hour training (provided free). Monitors visit facilities quarterly, document conditions, and write public reports. Currently, 12 volunteers support this work; we need 30 to cover all 13 state prisons.

Our Drug Policy & Overdose Prevention program seeks peer outreach volunteers in Maricopa, Pima, and Coconino counties. You'll distribute naloxone and harm reduction supplies, connect people to treatment, and report policy barriers. This is boots-on-the-ground work requiring cultural humility and commitment to non-judgment. We provide training, supplies, and a $200 monthly stipend.

Training Sign-Ups: Build Your Advocacy Skills

The Core Civics & Advocacy Training for Returning Citizens spring 2025 cohort runs March–May, meeting Wednesdays 6–8 PM at our Phoenix office with hybrid options. Topics include navigating the Arizona Legislature, testifying effectively, working with media, and campaign planning. Applications close February 20. Priority goes to directly impacted individuals; allies may apply for limited slots. The $500 stipend is distributed in two installments.

Our ARTS in Prison & Think Motion program offers teaching artist training for formerly incarcerated creatives. This 8-week course prepares you to facilitate workshops inside ADCRR facilities. Graduates are eligible for paid teaching positions. Next session begins April 1. We cover all materials and provide mentorship from experienced arts staff.

For organizations, we offer customized training on engaging directly impacted communities, trauma-informed advocacy, and the Seven Ps framework. We've trained staff at Phoenix Legal Action Network, Pima County Public Defender, and ACLU of Arizona. Request training via our contact form.
